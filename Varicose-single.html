Varicose veins

Varicose veins are swollen, twisted veins that can occur anywhere in the body, but are most common in the legs. They usually develop when the valves in the veins become weak or damaged, allowing blood to pool and the veins to bulge.

Symptoms of varicose veins

  • Bulging, bluish veins
  • Aching or heaviness in the legs
  • Fatigue
  • Itching or burning around the veins
  • Muscle cramps
  • Swelling in the ankles and feet
  • Skin changes, such as darkening or dryness

Causes of Varicose veins

  • Family history
  • Age
  • Pregnancy
  • Hormonal changes
  • Obesity
  • Standing or sitting for long periods of time
  • Wearing tight clothing
